​

The Board of Directors constitute the governing body of ICCC. It is composed of 10 Directors elected by the voting members in good standing. Each Director holds a term of two (2) years.

​

The Directors are responsible for the development, implementation, and management of the policies and procedures of the ICCC. As part of the governing body, they have supervision, control, and direction of the affairs of ICCC, its committees and publications. They determine policies of the Chamber or changes therein, promote its objectives, and supervise the disbursement of its funds.

​

The Board may adopt such rules and regulations for the conduct of its business as it is advisable, and may, in the execution of the powers granted, delegate certain authority and responsibility to the Secretary-General and full-time staff of ICCC.

​

The Directors meet once a month. Nevertheless, special meetings of the Board of Directors or the Executive Board of Directors may be called whenever necessary as provided for in the ICCC by law.
